  • HP moves out of the garage

    HP started off in a one-car garage with nothing but the idea of the future. This company was started by Bill Hewlett and David Packward. Bill Hewlett and David Packward both graduated with degrees in electrical engineering at Stanford University in 1935. HP started with a capital investment of $538, which is equivalent to $9,772 in 2019. Their company went public on November 6, 1957.

  • HP goes public

    Hewkett Packward went public on November 6, 1957 after only starting the company in 1947.

  • HP goes global

    Sales began to increase exponentially that they decided to go global after reaching 5.5 million dollars. They decided to move their organization to Geneva. During the 1960s, they partnered with Sony to develop high-quality products. Those products were a huge cost to the company, as they were not a huge success.

  • HP goes to the Medical Field

    After the sales of the HP computer grew exponentially, hospitals and clinics decided that it would be be wise and more efficient to input all of the information for patients and work off of the computer. This meant that HP made it into the medical field! This purchase from the medical department really opened more opportunities for the company as they began to be sold all over the world.

  • HP makes Fortune List

    After the massive amount of success economically and publicity, HP finally made the Fortune 500 list in 1963. This was a huge accomplishment for HP and they were one of the first technical/computer related company to make it.
